# Professional Portrait

**Purpose:** Transform an existing photograph into a professional portrait while preserving the person's identity with maximum accuracy. The result should look captured by an experienced lifestyle portrait photographer — not generated by AI.

**When to run:** A photo needs to become a professional portrait — LinkedIn, company websites, speaker profiles, press kits, personal branding.

**Inputs:** Subject photograph(s); background image; optional preferences for pose, framing, mood, clothing, expression, placement (all have defaults — see Run Sequence).

**Output:** One validated portrait image, delivered per the Output Spec.

**Skills called:** none

**Version:** 1.3

---

# PURPOSE

Typical destinations: LinkedIn, company websites, speaker profiles, press kits, personal branding.

The final image should look like it was captured by an experienced lifestyle portrait photographer—not generated by AI.

This is **not** a corporate headshot.

The goal is a portrait suitable for professionals in creative industries, technology, startups, design, consulting, media, and leadership.

The visual style should feel:

- Relaxed
- Modern
- Premium
- Approachable
- Effortlessly stylish

Think:

- Creative Director
- Product Designer
- Startup Founder
- UX Lead
- Photographer
- Architect
- Creative Technologist

rather than:

- Banker
- Lawyer
- Insurance Agent

# CORE PRINCIPLE

Identity is sacred.

Improve the presentation.

Never redesign the person.

The portrait should immediately be recognizable by friends, family and colleagues.

Priority Order:

1. Facial Identity
2. Facial Expression
3. Hairstyle
4. Glasses & Accessories
5. Body Proportions
6. Pose
7. Clothing
8. Lighting
9. Background Integration

Never change this order.

---

# REQUIRED INPUTS

## 1. Subject Photograph

Upload the photograph containing the person.

Questions:

- Is this the primary reference image?
- Are there additional photos of the person?

If multiple photographs are provided:

- Use one image for composition.
- Use every image for facial reference.

More references = higher facial accuracy.

---

## 2. Background

Upload the desired background.

Supported:

- Pattern
- Illustration
- Brand artwork
- Gradient
- Lifestyle photograph
- Environmental photograph
- Office interior
- Architectural background
- Texture

The background should remain visually identical.

Do not redesign or reinterpret it.

Only adjust:

- Perspective
- Scale
- Lighting
- Focus
- Depth of field

---

## 3. Pose Preference

Choose one.

Default: **B — Refine Pose**

### A — Preserve Pose

Maintain the original body position.

Only improve posture naturally.

---

### B — Refine Pose

Maintain the overall pose while making it feel:

- more relaxed
- more confident
- more balanced

No dramatic repositioning.

---

### C — New Pose

Create an entirely new pose that better complements the chosen background.

Examples:

- One hand pocket
- Both hands pockets
- Arms relaxed
- Leaning slightly
- Half turn
- Sitting naturally
- Walking naturally
- Standing casually

# RUN SEQUENCE

Every run follows this order. No step is skipped.

## 1 — Intake

Collect:

- Subject photograph(s)
- Background image
- Pose preference (default: B — Refine Pose)
- Framing (default: Waist Up)
- Mood (default: Casual Chic)
- Clothing (default: Preserve)
- Expression instruction (optional; default: Expression Lock applies)

If the subject provides no preference for an input,
apply the default and proceed.

Do not stall the run with questions
the defaults already answer.

## 2 — Reference Resolution

If multiple subject photographs are provided:

- Designate one as the **composition reference**.
- All others are **facial references** only.

Conflict rule:

The composition reference wins on all mutable attributes—
glasses, hairstyle, hair length, facial hair, accessories.

Facial references inform bone structure,
proportions, and skin detail only.

## 3 — Confirm

Before generating, state back in one block:

- Composition reference
- Pose / Framing / Mood / Clothing selections
- Any defaults applied

Proceed unless corrected.

## 4 — Generate

Produce one candidate portrait.

One at a time.

Not a grid.

Identity accuracy degrades when
generation is treated as a lottery.

## 5 — Validate

Run the Final Validation Checklist in order.

Identity section first.

If Identity fails, stop—
do not evaluate the remaining sections.

Go to Retry Rules.

## 6 — Deliver or Iterate

All sections pass → deliver.

Any section fails → apply Retry Rules.

Maximum three retry cycles per run.

After three failures, stop and report
which constraint is unachievable
with the current inputs.

Do not silently relax the Identity Lock
to force a completion.

# OUTPUT SPEC

State the intended placement before generating.

### Circle-Crop Avatar

(LinkedIn profile, Slack, GitHub, most platform avatars)

- Renders as a **circle**.
- Compose square-safe: face centred,
  generous headroom, nothing critical
  in the corners.
- Waist Up remains valid, but the face
  must survive a centre circle crop.

### Landscape Placement

(Banners, website headers, press features)

- Landscape orientation.
- Subject off-centre is acceptable.

### General Professional

(Speaker profiles, about pages, press kits)

- Deliver at the native aspect ratio
  of the composition.
- No forced crop.

Default placement:

**General Professional**

If the destination is unknown,
compose circle-safe anyway—
it costs nothing and survives
every placement.

The face must remain as close to the uploaded photograph as possible.

Do NOT reinterpret.

Do NOT beautify.

Do NOT stylize.

Preserve:

- Face shape
- Jawline
- Forehead
- Hairline
- Hair direction
- Hairstyle
- Eye shape
- Eye spacing
- Eye size
- Eyelids
- Eyebrows
- Nose
- Lips
- Smile
- Teeth visibility
- Cheeks
- Chin
- Ears
- Skin texture
- Wrinkles
- Moles
- Freckles
- Facial hair
- Glasses
- Age
- Weight
- Ethnicity

The goal is:

"A better photograph of the exact same person."

Not

"A prettier version."

---

# EXPRESSION LOCK

Unless an expression instruction was given at intake:

Maintain the exact facial expression.

Preserve:

- Smile intensity
- Eye openness
- Eye direction
- Lip position
- Eyebrow position
- Relaxation
- Emotion

Do not:

- Increase smile
- Add confidence smirk
- Change emotion
- Open mouth
- Close mouth
- Raise eyebrows

The expression should feel identical.

Only photographed under better lighting.

# BACKGROUND INTEGRATION

Blend the subject naturally into the uploaded background.

Allowed:

- Matching colour temperature
- Matching perspective
- Matching focus
- Matching light direction
- Matching exposure

Not allowed:

- Redesigning the background
- Replacing background elements
- Changing the artwork
- Altering the pattern

---

# COLOUR GRADING

Natural.

Realistic.

Premium.

Avoid:

- Over saturation
- Orange skin
- Artificial skin smoothing
- Excessive sharpening
- Heavy contrast

Skin should retain natural imperfections.

# RETRY RULES

Failures are fixed by reducing degrees of freedom,
never by regenerating blindly.

The generator has a freedom budget.

Every liberty granted—new pose, new clothing,
new lighting environment—spends budget
that would otherwise protect the face.

When a run fails, refund freedom
in this order:

## Identity Failure

The face drifted.

1. Downgrade Clothing to **Preserve**.
2. Downgrade Pose to **A — Preserve Pose**.
3. Retry.
4. Still failing → request additional facial
   reference photographs before any further attempt.

Never fix identity drift by
"trying again with the same settings."

Same settings produce the same drift.

## Expression Failure

Face is right, emotion is wrong.

1. Re-anchor to the composition reference expression.
2. Explicitly restate the Expression Lock
   in the generation instruction.
3. Retry with all other settings unchanged.

## Body / Hands Failure

Anatomy or hands are wrong.

1. Simplify the pose—hands in pockets
   or fully out of frame.
2. Tighten framing one level
   (Waist Up → Chest Up) if hands
   remain unfixable.
3. Retry.

## Environment Failure

Background was redesigned or the blend is off.

1. Restate the background as immutable input.
2. Correct only integration parameters:
   perspective, colour temperature,
   focus, light direction.
3. Retry.

## Priority Under Conflict

If fixing one failure would cause another,
resolve by the Priority Order.

Identity always wins.

A portrait with a perfect background
and the wrong face is a failed run.

A portrait with a slightly awkward
background blend and the right face
is a fixable run.

# SUCCESS CRITERIA

If someone who personally knows the subject sees the portrait, their reaction should be:

> "That's a really great photo of you."

—not—

> "That doesn't quite look like you."

The objective is to elevate the photography, never to alter the person's identity.
